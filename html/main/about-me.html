About Me

My name is Benjamin Waterson. I recently finished up five years at CU Boulder, studying psychology, music, and TAM (technology, arts, and media). I grew up near Oakland, California, and I currently live near Boulder, Colorado. I'm primarily a drumset player, but I also have experience with piano and alto saxophone. I played alto in the CU marching band for 4 years, and I played drumset in the CU basketball for 4 years as well. Nowadays I play drums for Just Another Mountain Band and record drum tracks out of a home studio here in Colorado. I update my blog every Sunday, be sure to check it out!

More Info:

How long have you been playing music for?

All in all, about 18 years. I took piano lessons for a few years when I was real young, starting around age 6. I had my first drum lesson in August of 2005 at the age of 8, and I had private instruction for exactly ten years — my final lesson was when I moved out in August of 2015. I picked up the sax when I started college that year, and the rest is history. In fact, I’m getting misty-eyed just thinking about it.

What kind of drums do you play?

I have a Pearl set for the studio, although sadly Pearl doesn’t make the exact model anymore… such is life. This kit has Sabian cymbals, and I always use Remo heads and Vic Firth sticks. I have a variety of doodads from LP and Index Drums. I use DW hardware — when I can afford it.


If you see me drum live, you’ll probably spot me with an old Ludwig kit I got for out of the house playing. I fitted this one with some second hand Zildjian cymbals.

What DAW do you use?

I do pretty much everything in Logic Pro. I also make good use of Max Signal Processing, a bit of SuperCollider, and the amazing PaulStretch. I have Isotope’s Rx7 if I need it, as well as the plugins that came with my UA interface (like fancy compressors and oxidizers). Most of the time, I just use what’s built into Logic. Oh, I also have Audacity, like everyone else.

What do you use to create music notation?

I use MuseScore for all my transcribing/engraving. It’s pretty glitchy, but with a bit of patience, I can pretty much do anything I want. I really admire the commitment the developers have to maintain MuseScore while keeping it free; I have no reason to shell out for Finale or Sibelius.

Who are your favorite drummers?

For first place, we have a tie between John Bonham and Keith Moon. There’s about a 30 way tie for second place…

What has been your most difficult transcription?

Right now, probably Tommy Igoe’s solo from “Jazz Crimes”, off of the 2014 album The Tommy Igoe Groove Conspiracy. I haven’t shared it yet because it needs several more hours of proofreading. It'll show up sometime early next year. Until then, stay tuned…
